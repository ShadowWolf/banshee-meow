Our Story

Bryan and Melissa first got to know each other through a mutual friend (also named Brian) one weekend in Madison, WI in February 2013. The two of them kept in touch and occasionally even played some of the same games! Melissa eventually made a major move from Rochester, MN up to St. Paul, MN for a new job working for the local newspaper. Through a funny twist of fate, Bryan managed to land a job in the same advertising field in the Minneapolis area while living in Kansas City, MO and relocated. Both being new to the area, they started to hang out a lot more and the obvious connection between them kept blooming.

The two of them officially began dating starting with a Twin’s game in early June 2014. This began the trend of Melissa making several long trips from St. Paul to the North Loop in Minneapolis on many weekends, often carrying her desktop computer for impromptu LAN sessions.

As fall turned into winter, Bryan and Melissa knew they had something special when they could stand being in the same room despite cheering for different hockey and football teams! (Wild/Packers – Melissa, Blackhawks/Bears – Bryan. It’s a fun household.) They continued to share experiences, family and hockey games until they decided to move in together to a nice apartment in the North Loop.

Bryan nad Melissa decided, after living in the north loop for a year, that they wanted to try living in a townhome and get away from the apartment style homes. They found an awesome place in Saint Louis Park that gave them a bit of the city mixed with a bit of the suburbs. It was there they decided to try a bit more of was dubbed "Adulting" together and found their tastes and opinion meshed very well. Their home also added a new member, Banshee. They quickly learned Banshee does not have interests or desires but obsessions and an unending need for play.

While in Saint Louis Park the topic of what the future held came up several times. They decided to go check out a few places for a ring, but often felt they weren't getting the type of thing they were looking for.

Out of Town Visitors

Minneapolis/St Paul is home to an amazing public transportation system. Simply use their service on the website to find the best way to get where you're looking to go: Metro Transit Website

If you are looking for food, entertainment, or sight seeing places, we recommend checking out some of these places

  • Minnehaha Regional Park (one of the nicest parks in the area)
  • Blue Door Pub (Home of the Blucy - worth checking out!)
  • Mill City Museum (former home of the world's largest flour mill)
  • Harriet Island on the Mississippi River
  • The Happy Gnome (amazing beer and bloody mary selection)
  • Hell's Kitchen (Not related to Gordon Ramsay but still about as loud and awesome)
  • Forepaughs (our favorite happy hour place)
